understanding the work

What Is a Death Doula?

A death doula is a non-medical companion for the end-of-life journey. I am not a doctor, a nurse, or a therapist. I don't diagnose, prescribe, or treat. What I offer is something the medical system was never designed to hold, presence. Spiritual companionship. The kind of deep, unhurried attention that allows a person to feel truly seen as they approach the threshold between this world and whatever comes next.

This work exists in the space between what medicine can do and what the soul needs. Hospice cares for the body. I tend to everything else, the fears spoken at 3 a.m., the legacy that wants to be named, the family that doesn't know how to say goodbye, the sacred geometry of a life preparing to transform.

This work is for the dying, yes. But it is equally for the living, for the partner who can't sleep, the daughter carrying guilt, the friend who wants to help but doesn't know how. Death asks something of all of us. I help you answer.

The Gathering

Early conversations, planning, and preparation. Naming what matters most.

The Deepening

Active companionship as the journey unfolds. Legacy work, vigil planning, family support.

The Threshold

Sacred presence during the final hours and the moment of passing.

The Tending

Grief support, memorial guidance, and gentle accompaniment forward.

The Integration

Finding your footing. Learning to carry love in its new form.

Not medical care

I do not replace doctors, nurses, or hospice workers. I work alongside them, filling the spaces that medical care cannot reach, the emotional, the spiritual, the deeply personal. Think of me as the person who tends the hearth while others tend the body.

Not therapy

While I hold space for enormous feelings, I am not a licensed counselor. What I offer is companionship, the kind that doesn't pathologize grief or try to fix what isn't broken. Sometimes the most healing thing is simply being witnessed.

For everyone

You don't need to be dying to benefit from this work. If you are caring for someone who is, if you carry unresolved grief, if the thought of death keeps you awake, this work is for you. There is no prerequisite for seeking tenderness.

what I offer

Offerings

End-of-Life Companionship

being present, fully

This is the heart of the work. I sit with you, or with your person, through the dying process. Not to manage it, but to honor it. I bring calm into chaos, spiritual grounding into uncertainty, and a steady hand to hold when the world feels like it's tilting. We move at the pace of what is needed, never faster.

Legacy & Life Review

honoring the story of a life

Every life is a library. Before the final chapter closes, I help your person reflect on, name, and preserve what mattered most. This might look like recording their stories, writing letters to loved ones, creating memory books, or simply having the conversations that have waited too long. What we create together becomes a gift that outlasts a lifetime.

Vigil Planning

designing a sacred space

The final days and the moment of passing deserve more than a hospital room on autopilot. Together, we design a vigil that reflects who your person truly is, the music, the scents, the lighting, the prayers or poems, the people present. We create a container of beauty and intention so the transition is held, not just endured.

Grief Tending

not fixing, just tending

Grief is not a problem to solve. It is love with nowhere to go, and it will find its way if we let it. After a loss, I offer companionship for the living. Not timelines, not stages, not forced silver linings. Just a safe place to fall apart and a gentle hand as you learn to carry what remains. Like tending a garden after a storm, we don't rush the blooming.

Every journey is different. These are starting points, not rigid packages.

From Bailey's Journal

I have learned that the most sacred thing I can offer is my silence, my breath, my willingness to be fully here while someone I love steps fully there. This is the work. It is not easy. It is the most important thing I have ever done.
